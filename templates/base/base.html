Generations

Generation I

The origin of Pokémon is in two games called Poketto Monsutā Midori, better known as Pokémon Green, and Poketto Monsuttā Aka, known in the west as Pokémon Red, released in 1996, in Japan. Pokémon Green was never released outside of Japan, being released only Pokémon Red Version and Blue Version. Pokémon Blue was also released in Japan a few months after the first two were released. Due to the success of these games, which became some of the best-selling games in Japan in 1996,[13] a different version was produced, called Pokémon Yellow: Special Pikachu Edition; had as main Pokémon a Pikachu that was always following the player out of his Poké Ball. Pokémon Red & Blue has sold over ten million copies in the West and Pokémon Yellow has sold over five million copies alone.

Generation II

As it gained more and more fans worldwide, the creators decided to make a sequel to the first games. This generated in two new versions, which brought an adventure different from the previous ones, located in a Region next to the stage of the first games, 100 new Pokémon, totaling 251, new features, among others. Known as Pokémon Gold & Silver, they served as the basis for the mechanism of the following ones, presenting the distinction between male and female Pokémon, enabling the crossing of Pokémon, a concept often used for exchanges between players, using time within the game, differentiating the day of the night and many other news for players.

Generation III

In March 2003, the first third-generation games were released, better known to gamers as the Advance generation, as their original RPGs were made for the Game Boy Advance handheld: Pokémon Ruby & Sapphire, which surpassed 3.1 million units sold, thus being the most commercialized in Japan in 2002[16] brought 135 new Pokémon, but it was impossible to transfer Pokémon from previous generations to the games, as their training in Pokémon Ruby & Sapphire was based on another system, that of Effort Values ​​(effort values), points that, when well calculated and distributed to the Pokémon, made it stronger than others trained without this method. In order to get some of the older Pokémon, Pokémon Ruby & Sapphire would link up with other third-gen games, most notably Pokémon Colosseum, one of that generation's platform-type games.

Generation IV

In September 2006, the first two versions of original RPGs of fourth generation Pokémon, made for the Nintendo DS, were released in Japan. Pokémon Diamond & Pearl introduced 106 new Pokémon, totaling 493, new functions, such as a change in the attacks part, dividing them into three types (Physical, which uses the Attack value; Special, which uses the Special Attack value and Other, which uses any other value), the return of time changes between day and night, last used in Pokémon Gold & Silver, including the ability to connect online through Nintendo Wi-Fi Connection, where players from anywhere on the world could battle and trade Pokémon with various other players on the planet. Also introduced in Nintendo DS the use of a headset that allowed conversations between players who would face off on the Nintendo WFC. Pokémon Diamond & Pearl sold over 1.58 million copies in its first four days in Japan[21] and were also the country's best-selling games for the entire year of 2006.[22] In the United States Pokémon Diamond & Pearl sold more than one million copies in the first week of sales[23] and more than ten worldwide.

Sobre Nós

Olá, estamos no último semestre de informática do IFSC Campus Gaspar e fizemos este projeto com o objetivo de formar e informar os leitores sobre o que são ações afirmativas, quais os seus papéis na sociedade e que ações temos em nosso câmpus. Esperamos esclarecer as dúvidas e aprofundar seus conhecimentos sobre o assunto. Caso possuam alguma dúvida ou queiram conversar sobre o assunto estamos todos à disposição para ajudar.
